Resume Building

Write a Resume That Gets Noticed

The Golden Rules of a Great Resume

Your resume is your first impression. Recruiters spend an average of 6–7 seconds scanning it. Make every second count with these proven techniques.

  • Use a clean, single-column layout — readability beats creativity
  • Lead with a 2-line professional summary highlighting your top skill
  • Quantify achievements — "Increased app downloads by 40%" beats "Improved app"
  • Tailor keywords to the job description to pass ATS filters
  • Keep it to 1 page if you have under 3 years of experience

Key Topics

Your Complete Career Toolkit

Resume

Craft a Winning Resume

  • Use action verbs: Built, Designed, Led, Increased
  • Include a GitHub / portfolio link for tech roles
  • List relevant skills in a dedicated section
  • Proofread — one typo can cost you the interview
Interview

Ace Every Interview

  • Research the company thoroughly before the call
  • Prepare STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) examples
  • Have 3 thoughtful questions ready to ask the interviewer
  • Dress professionally even for video interviews
Networking

Build Your Network

  • Optimise your LinkedIn profile with a clear headline
  • Connect with alumni from your college — they want to help
  • Engage with industry content regularly to stay visible
  • Attend virtual career fairs and webinars
Cover Letter

Write Cover Letters That Work

  • Personalise every letter — never send a generic one
  • Open with a compelling hook, not "I am applying for..."
  • Connect your specific skills to the role's requirements
  • Keep it under 250 words — brevity signals confidence
Skills

Skills That Employers Want

  • Learn at least one cloud platform: AWS, Firebase, or GCP
  • Communication and teamwork matter as much as technical skills
  • Build public projects — open-source contributions shine
  • Stay current: follow industry blogs and newsletters
Salary

Negotiate with Confidence

  • Research market rates on Glassdoor and LinkedIn Salary
  • Always negotiate — 70% of employers expect candidates to
  • Consider the full package: stipend, equity, learning budget
  • Practice your negotiation script with a friend
